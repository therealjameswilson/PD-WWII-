# Gap Remediation Plan: FRUS Public Diplomacy, World War II

This remediation pass converts the gap register into compiler infrastructure.
It does not claim that the archival gaps are closed. The new status is
**Mitigating** because the next step is to prove each row with source copies,
folder/file identifiers, author-recipient metadata, and FRUS selection
rationale.

## Added Remediation Layers

1. **Document-intake ledger.** Eighteen rows now route the first harvest by
   repository, record group, source-pool ID, candidate type, selection test,
   source-copy task, and gap IDs.

2. **Selection rules.** Twelve rules define when to promote, hold, or exclude
   candidate material. The rules prevent the volume from becoming a media-output
   inventory or domestic morale history.

3. **Coverage matrix.** Ten theater/audience rows track Europe, neutral Europe,
   Latin America, China-Burma-India, the Pacific, the Middle East and North
   Africa, the Soviet/Eastern Europe lane, global radio, Washington control, and
   supplemental reception.

4. **Gap remediation notes.** Each of the 15 original gaps now records the
   intake rows, rules, or coverage rows that mitigate it.

## Harvest Order

1. **RG 59 first.** Locate State Department clearance, objections,
   instructions, MacLeish/public-cultural affairs files, and IIIS handoff
   records before treating OWI media files as FRUS candidates.

2. **RG 84 next.** Build post matrices for London, Lisbon, Madrid, Stockholm,
   Ankara, Cairo, Moscow, Chungking, Mexico City, Rio de Janeiro, Buenos Aires,
   Santiago, Bogota, Havana, and other priority posts.

3. **RG 208 overseas core.** Mine OWI Overseas Operations, outposts,
   communications, New York, San Francisco, publications, radio, and field
   reporting files.

4. **VOA/radio sub-ledger.** Link scripts, language desks, shortwave
   facilities, clearance, monitoring, OIAA radio, Office of Censorship logs, and
   embassy reaction.

5. **FDR/White House public-line binder.** Tie Four Freedoms, Pearl Harbor,
   1943 State of the Union, D-Day, United Nations, surrender, and victory themes
   to drafts, routing, treatment, clearance, and reception.

6. **Boundary review.** Keep OCIAA operational programs, domestic morale,
   military psychological warfare, and supplemental/private sources outside the
   main chronology unless the selection rules promote them.

## Proof Needed Before Any Final FRUS Selection

- Date
- Author
- Recipient
- Document type
- Repository
- Record group or collection
- Box/file, folder, catalog identifier, or finding-aid locator
- Source-copy status
- Selection rationale
- Linked public-line, policy, implementation, and reaction evidence where
  applicable
