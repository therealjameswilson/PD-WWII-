# Compiler Gap Analysis: FRUS Public Diplomacy, World War II

This is a gap register for compiling a retro FRUS volume, not a website feature
check. The current assist page is a useful source map, but the volume still
needs a document-level selection system that can support FRUS editorial
judgment: policy significance, diplomatic activity, source-copy provenance,
chronological balance, and clear boundaries around propaganda and domestic
morale.

## Priority Gaps

1. **No document-level selection register.** The official History.state.gov page
   still lists the volume as being researched, and the current page is organized
   around source pools. The next step is an item-level intake ledger with date,
   author, recipient, document type, repository, box/file or catalog ID,
   source-copy status, and selection rationale.

2. **State Department records are underrepresented.** A FRUS volume cannot rest
   mainly on OWI agency records. RG 59 searches need to cover Central Files,
   Archibald MacLeish, public information, cultural affairs, and the 1945
   transfer to State.

3. **Foreign Service post reporting is missing.** Public diplomacy happened
   abroad. RG 84 post records should test Washington decisions against embassy
   implementation and host-government or audience reaction.

4. **OWI overseas outposts are not mapped.** RG 208 must be mined for outpost,
   New York, San Francisco, communications, overseas publications, radio, and
   field-reporting records.

5. **VOA and radio need their own policy chain.** The volume needs guidance,
   scripts, language desks, shortwave facilities, clearance, monitoring, and
   reaction, not just a VOA source-pool label.

6. **OCIAA needs boundary discipline.** RG 229 information, radio, film,
   education, press, publications, and graphics records belong in scope.
   Transportation, health, agriculture, and economic development records belong
   in boundary status unless they directly document information strategy.

7. **State handoff files are still too thin.** The bridge from wartime OWI/OIAA
   to postwar State information programs needs direct RG 59 and Interim
   International Information Service records.

8. **FDR and White House files need folder-level candidates.** Speeches and
   public statements should be tied to drafts, routing, meetings, OWI treatment,
   State clearance, overseas guidance, and reception.

9. **Military information and psychological warfare need inclusion rules.** RG
   107 and RG 165 should be boundary pools unless records show interagency
   policy, foreign-audience strategy, diplomatic controversy, or direct
   OWI/State coordination.

10. **Domestic morale and public-opinion material needs selection rules.**
    Surveys and domestic operations can be evidence, but should enter the main
    chronology only when they shaped overseas information policy, foreign
    audiences, White House/State decisions, or diplomatic activity.

11. **Outputs are not tied to policy.** OWI/OIAA publications, films, posters,
    photographs, broadcasts, and exhibits should be linked to policy guidance,
    clearance, distribution, and foreign reaction before being treated as FRUS
    documents.

12. **Interwar and postwar boundaries are not operationalized.** Volume II
    covers the interwar period and Volume IV covers 1945-1952. This volume needs
    rules for 1939-1941 predecessor material and post-August 1945 transfer
    material.

13. **Persons and organization authority are too thin.** The persons list needs
    accurate titles, title dates, aliases, and office successions across OGR,
    OFF, COI, OWI, OIAA, State, War, and Foreign Service posts.

14. **Geographic and audience balance is not measurable.** The current source
    map does not yet track Europe, Latin America, China, the Pacific, the Middle
    East, neutral states, occupied territories, liberated areas, or language
    services.

15. **Supplemental sources are not scoped.** Private papers, Allied records,
    press, memoirs, and foreign reaction records can help find missing U.S.
    documentation, but should not replace official U.S. records in the main
    chronology.

## Immediate Harvest Order

1. Build the document-level intake ledger.
2. Add RG 59 and RG 84 searches before expanding more OWI media rows.
3. Mine RG 208 overseas/outpost/radio/communications records.
4. Build a separate VOA/radio sub-ledger.
5. Add FDR Library folder-level White House leads for major public-line events.
6. Treat RG 107, RG 165, domestic morale, and OCIAA non-information programs as
   boundary material unless they meet explicit public-diplomacy criteria.
